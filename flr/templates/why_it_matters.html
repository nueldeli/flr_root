Why does it matters?


1. To enrich logged-over areas with high value timber species

Sarawak is renowned with its timber resources and the forest diversity is vast with various species of high value timber. For the past decades, Sarawak forest has experienced degradation as the result of logging due to high global market demand for timber supply, not to mention the increasing number of illegal logging.

In order to prevent further degradation of land, those logged-over areas are restored with new species. Only this time, the new strategy is to fill the logged-over areas with high value timber species. This approach is vital to ensure the maximum productivity of forest products in the long run. At the same time, the approach preserves biodiversity and keeps the ecosystem in balance.


2. To restore and conserve degraded forests in Permanent Forest Estate (PFE) and State land

The Sarawak government has gazetted 6 million hectares of state forest as Permanent Forest Estate (PFE) whereas 1 million hectares has been designated as Totally Protected Area (TPA). PFE and TPA serve the sole purpose of sustainable forest management. In Sarawak, there are 3 areas of concern which are PFE, TPA and State Land.

All these designated areas had been degraded over time as the result of natural causes and human activities. The gazettement of PFE and State Land is the first initiative to bring back the degraded forests area into its greener state. After years of planning and execution, the most holistic and promising way to save the forest is through restoration and conservation effort.
